# Tenura Mission

Tenura exists to make affordable housing navigable — starting with Section 8,
expanding to every program in the `HousingAssistance` model. The platform is
the vehicle. The mission is housing.

## The Revenue Pledge

Revenue is not the drive. Revenue is what supports the drive.

The founder has set a personal compensation ceiling. Once operating costs are
covered and that ceiling is met, **100% of Tenura's net revenue is directed
toward building new affordable housing units.**

This is modeled on the one-for-one commitments that made Bombas (socks) and
Warby Parker (glasses) category-defining companies — except the product given
back is the same product the platform helps people find: a home.

## Why This Model

- **Alignment.** Every subscription, every transaction fee, every premium
  service sold on Tenura increases the number of affordable units that
  exist in the world. Growth of the business and growth of the supply of
  housing are the same curve.
- **Durability.** Capping founder upside makes the commitment credible to
  tenants, landlords, and capital partners. It is a structural promise, not
  a marketing line.
- **Focus.** The ceiling removes the incentive to optimize for extraction.
  Product decisions get judged against a single question: does this help
  more people get housed?

## What This Commits Us To

1. **Transparent accounting.** An Impact page in the app will publish, in
   near-real-time, how much revenue has been earned, how much has been
   directed to housing construction, and how many units that has funded or
   co-funded.
2. **Independent verification.** Housing contributions will be routed through
   a 501(c)(3) or equivalent partner so that reporting is auditable and not
   self-attested.
3. **Ceiling disclosure.** The founder's ceiling is a fixed, publicly
   disclosed number. It does not quietly rise with revenue.
4. **Mission lock.** Any future corporate restructuring (equity round,
   acquisition, etc.) must preserve this commitment or the mission has
   been abandoned, not evolved.

## What This Does *Not* Mean

- Tenura is not a charity. It is a real business with real operating costs,
  a real team, and real obligations to landlords and tenants on the platform.
- The housing commitment kicks in after costs and the founder ceiling — not
  before. A company that cannot sustain itself cannot fund anything.
- "Affordable housing" means units that meet HUD affordability standards or
  an equivalent local/state definition — not merely "cheaper than the median."

## Where This Lives in the Product

- **`/impact`** — public page summarizing the pledge, the ceiling, and

- **Landing page footer / About** — short version of the pledge linking to
  this document.
- **Annual impact post** — written summary each year, linked from `/impact`.
